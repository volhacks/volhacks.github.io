Frequently Asked Questions



Who can come?

All current undergraduate and graduate students at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville are guaranteed spots. Current undergraduate and undergraduate students at other universities are encouraged to register and we will try to accommodate as many attendees as possible.

Will there be free food, drinks, and caffeine?

Yes! Absolutely! We will provide catered meals, drinks at all times, snack breaks, and endless caffeine.

Where do I sleep?

We will have a designated quiet sleeping area on site, and you are encouraged to bring sleeping bags, pillows, toiletries, and anything else you need to sleep. There are no showers at the venue, so please be prepared to go without showering unless you have other accommodations.

When and where is VolHacks?

VolHacks will take place on September 30th to October 2nd, at the University of Tennessee, Knoxville in the foothills of the Great Smoky Mountains.

I don't know what to build — any ideas?

Don't worry at all! We will walk you through how to brainstorm great ideas at the beginning of VolHacks.

How do teams work?

There is a maximum of 4 people per team. Once the idea brainstorming session is done, there will be an open mic and networking time to find a team. Everyone registered will also be invited to the VolHacks Slack team prior to the hackathon in order to network and discuss ideas.

Is there a code of conduct?

Yes, we abide by the Major League Hacking Code of Conduct.

How much does it cost?

The event is completely free for eligible and registered students. We will provide you with the venue, food, Wi-Fi, etc. You just need to show up!

Do I need to stay the entire time?

Nope, we are not a lock-in. We realize that 36 hours is a long time, and that some people might have other commitments during that time. Feel free to stay and leave as you choose. However, we would prefer that people stay to promote the environment.

Where do I park?

If you're on campus already and have a parking permit, park where you would normally park. For attendees coming from other universities, we will distribute information to registered attendees prior to the event regarding where to park.

I've never been to a hackathon before, what should I do?

No worries at all. We will be providing mentors during the hackathon and running workshops, as well as providing food and drinks and a place to sleep if you don't have one. We will also have a Slack team for anyone to get help at during the hackathon.

What should I bring?

You will definitely need your laptop, phone, their chargers, as well as a valid student or government ID. Feel free to also bring along a sleeping bag, and we recommend some comfortable clothes.

What kind of things can we hack up? Are there any limits?

You are not limited to what you can make, hack, or build for the hackathon EXCEPT for the following restrictions:

  • It cannot be illegal in any way
  • It cannot go against the MLH code of conduct
  • It cannot compromise the integrity of the UTK networks, resources, faculty, or staff
  • It cannot go against the UTK code of student conduct (find that here)
